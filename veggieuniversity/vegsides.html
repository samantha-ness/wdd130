Did you know...

"A serving of cucumber -- one-half a cup -- is about 8 calories"

(see "Cucumber" from WebMD)

  • Half of a medium or large cucumber, or a small whole one
  • Feta cheese
  • Your favorite vinaigrette dressing
This idea is a spin-off of/was inspired by this Greek Salad Stuffed Cucumber Bites recipe from the website Divalicious Recipes--feel free to check it out if you have a little more time to cook!

For this simplified version, wash and chop up your cucumber in slices or quarters, however you prefer (if you want tips on how to quarter a cucumber, visit the Tips & Tricks page). Then drizzle with the vinaigrette dressing, sprinkle with some feta cheese and enjoy your cucumber salad!

  • Half of a medium or large cucumber, or a small whole one
  • ½-1 tbsp soy sauce to taste
  • Powdered ginger
  • Garlic powder (but if you have fresh garlic/ginger on hand use that!)
  • Chili powder or flakes (if desired/on hand)
Wash your cucumber and then slice or cut into quarters, (see the Tips and Tricks page if you don't know how to quarter a cucumber) then set it aside. In a bowl or container combine the soy sauce and spices and mix until well combined. Add in the cucumber and then stir/toss until the sauce coats the cucumber. Enjoy!

  • Half of a medium or large cucumber, or a small whole one
  • 2 tbsp sour cream or plain greek yogurt
  • ½ tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
Wash and slice your cucumber. In a small/dip-sized bowl or container, mix together sour cream/yogurt, lemon juice, spices, and salt and pepper to taste. Enjoy!

Did you know?

You can get up to 73% of your daily necessary vitamin A from a half cup of carrots!

see "Carrots" from WebMD

  • Carrots (however many you'd like to eat)
  • Cream cheese, 1 tbsp or more*
  • Sweet chili sauce (look in the international section of your store) a little less than your amount of cream cheese*
*Amounts for these ingredients can vary depending on how much dip you want and whether you prefer more cream cheese or chili sauce flavor

Mix desired amounts of cream cheese and sweet chili sauce together, and eat with your carrots!

  • 15 or so baby carrots
  • Drizzle of honey (maybe about a teaspoon)
  • 2 tsp Water
  • Powdered ginger (to taste, but add at least 1 tsp)
  • Garlic powder
  • Salt
In a microwave safe bowl, add the honey, water, and ginger, garlic and salt to taste. A little chili powder, cayenne, or chili flakes would also taste great if you'd like and have them on hand. Mix together until combined, then add in carrots and mix/shake the bowl until the carrots are coated in the sauce. Then microwave for 2 minutes, check on them, then microwave for an additional 1-3 minutes depending on your microwave. Make sure to cover your bowl and use enough water, this will help your carrots cook faster and not get dry. You may want to/need to add more in between microwaving if your carrots are looking dry while cooking. Enjoy!

  • Carrots
  • Cream cheese
  • Your favorite salsa
I had this with chips one time at my mission president's house, but this would taste great with carrots too! Simply mix the desired amount of cream cheese and salsa together, then enjoy with your carrots.

Did you know?

Zucchini is a great source of antioxidants!

(see this article from healthline )

  • Half of a zucchini
  • Garlic powder (to taste, about 1 tsp)
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Parmesan cheese
Wash and slice half of a zucchini, then place in a microwave safe container. Add salt, garlic powder, a drizzle of olive oil, then stir slightly to mix it a bit. Microwave for 45 seconds, then stir, then put it back in for 10 to 20 seconds depending on your microwave and how tender you want your zucchini. Then take the zucchini out, and sprinkle with some parmesan cheese. Stir, and enjoy--be careful, it'll be hot!

  • Half of a zucchini
  • 1 heaping teaspoon taco seasoning
  • Olive oil
  • Shredded cheese (Mexican style or similar)
Wash and slice a half of a zucchini, then put in a microwave safe container. Sprinkle on the taco seasoning, a drizzle of olive oil, then stir slightly to mix it a bit. Microwave for 45 seconds, then stir, then put it back in for 10 to 20 seconds depending on your microwave and how tender you want your zucchini. Then take the zucchini out and sprinkle with roughly a tablespoon of cheese. Stir, and enjoy--be careful, it'll be hot!

  • Half zucchini
  • ½ tbsp of pesto (more or less if you prefer)
Wash and slice a half of a zucchini, then put in a microwave safe container with a sprinkle of water to help it steam, and microwave for 45 seconds. Stir, then put it back in for 10 to 20 seconds depending on your microwave and how tender you want your zucchini. Once it's done, top with a dollop of your favorite pesto! If you'd like, you can pop it back in the microwave for a few seconds to warm up the pesto as well. Be careful as zucchini tends to get very hot since it has more moisture.
